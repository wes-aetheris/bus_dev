OGI Inspection Proof of Concept V1.0

Physics-Enhanced Thermal Imaging Solution for Oil & Gas Applications

Executive Summary

This Proof of Concept validates our physics-enhanced thermal imaging solution's capability to provide quantitative gas detection using uncooled sensors. The PoC will demonstrate technical feasibility of core performance claims and assess market interest for potential pilot deployment programs. Our objective is to test whether physics-informed AI can successfully bridge the detection gap between qualitative and quantitative OGI systems while achieving breakthrough pricing compared to traditional $100K+ cooled solutions. The four-week validation program will confirm our solution's ability to deliver quantitative measurement capabilities at $27K per monitoring point, representing a 5x cost reduction versus existing quantitative OGI systems. Success will establish the foundation for advancing to customer pilot programs and commercial development.

PoC Objectives

Technical Validation

  • Methane Detection Threshold: 15-20 g/h at 6m distance (conservative PoC goal, stretch goal 8-12 g/h)
  • Temperature Accuracy: ±1.2°C under controlled conditions (PoC target vs ±0.8-1.2°C long-term target)
  • False Alarm Rate: Demonstrate reduction from 30-60% baseline to 15-25% under typical outdoor industrial conditions (targeting <15% in steady-state operations)
  • Environmental Compensation: Validate physics-based approach across humidity, wind, altitude, temperature, and pressure variations
  • Detection Range: Confirm 6m performance baseline for commercial development pathway
  • Continuous Operation: 2.5+ hour stable operation periods

Business Validation

  • Customer Interest Assessment: Evaluate operator receptivity to $27K quantitative solution vs $35K-100K+ alternatives
  • Performance Gap Validation: Confirm 2-3x improvement in detection sensitivity over standard uncooled sensors
  • Cost Feasibility: Validate component costs can achieve target $27K per monitoring point system cost
  • Operational Workflow: Assess integration with existing inspection processes and training requirements
  • Competitive Positioning: Document performance advantages vs FLIR GF77/GF320 solutions during field testing
  • Regulatory Pathway: Establish EPA Method 21 compatibility for pilot deployment advancement

Risk Mitigation Strategies
PoC Technical Risks
  • Detection Performance Shortfall → Multiple test scenarios under controlled and field conditions, backup algorithm approaches
  • Environmental Compensation Inadequate → Extended condition testing across temperature/humidity ranges, algorithm refinement protocols
  • System Reliability Issues → Backup equipment procurement, comprehensive troubleshooting protocols, vendor support agreements
  • Component Cost Overruns → Alternative component sourcing, manufacturing cost analysis, value engineering assessment
PoC Market Risks
  • Customer Disinterest → Multiple operator engagement sessions, clear value proposition communication, competitive demonstration
  • Technical Approach Infeasible → Alternative physics modeling approaches, scope adjustment protocols, pivot strategies
  • Regulatory Barriers → Early EPA Method 21 compliance assessment, regulatory consultation, alternative pathway development
  • Competitive Response → Accelerated development timeline, IP protection assessment, first-mover advantage establishment
