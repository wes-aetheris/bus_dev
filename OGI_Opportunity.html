PoC Scope & Limitations

PoC Focus Areas

  • Quantitative Thermal Measurement: Physics-enhanced accuracy for temperature quantification and gas quantity estimation (not species identification). Initial PoC will use broadband LWIR filters optimized for signal-to-noise ratio rather than precise spectroscopy.
  • Methane Detection Sensitivity: Conservative goal: 15-20 g/h methane at 6m distance (beats current median by 25-30%, achievable with physics-based environmental compensation). Stretch goal: 8-12 g/h methane at 6m (significant industry improvement, requires advanced modeling).
  • General Gas Leak Detection: Detection and quantification of thermal signatures from gas emissions
  • False Alarm Reduction: Environmental compensation and noise filtering algorithms (reducing 30-60% false positives to 15-25% under typical outdoor industrial conditions, targeting <15% in steady-state operations)
  • Cost-Effective Platform: Demonstrating superior performance at breakthrough pricing
  • Network Integration: Edge AI processing with automated diagnostic capabilities

Future Development Pathways

  • Specific Gas Identification: Integration with narrowband LWIR lenses for methane, SF6, NH3, and other target gases
  • Multi-Gas Detection: Expansion to simultaneous detection of multiple gas types
  • Exquisite MWIR/T2SSL Detector Integration: Enhancement of ecosystem with high-performance mid-wave infrared and Type-II Superlattice detectors for superior spectral resolution, enhanced sensitivity, and extended detection ranges across challenging environmental conditions
  • Regulatory Certification: Validation and approval processes for compliance applications
  • Industry-Specific Solutions: Customized spectral configurations for different applications (oil & gas, chemical, petrochemical)
  • Spectroscopic Gas Identification: Dedicated spectral camera (Tau 2 + diffraction grating) for species identification and quantitative concentration measurement
  • Sensor Health Monitoring: Mapping physical deterioration processes of sensors as a function of noise pattern characteristics and performance degradation
