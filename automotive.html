  • Design-centric Over-engineering: Sensors designed for worst-case scenarios with excessive safety margins drive unnecessary costs
  • Schedule-driven Replacement: Fixed maintenance intervals based on statistical failure models rather than actual sensor condition create premature replacement waste
  • Multiple redundancy: Many high-trust applications deploy 2x sensors for the same function, including one-out-of-two (1oo2) sensor architectures[1]
  • No Fault Found (NFF): Research shows NFF events are "one of the major threats to overall reliability and customer satisfaction" in automotive[2] and are often sensor-caused
  • Industry spends $50B annually on warranty claims which are largely composed of sensor failures and replacements[6][7]

Solution Overview

Solution Framework Core Value Proposition: Transform static design-centric reliability into dynamic, physics-informed monitoring that predicts failures and optimizes performance in real-time.

  • Predictive Failure Analytics: Identify degradation patterns before failure occurs
  • Continuous Calibration: Maintain optimal sensor accuracy throughout lifecycle
  • Cybersecurity Monitoring: Detect and mitigate spurious signals and cyber threats
  • Root Cause Intelligence: Pinpoint precise failure mechanisms and sensor anomalies
  • Condition-Based Maintenance: Replace only when physics models indicate actual replacement is needed
