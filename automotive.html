  • Over-specification: Sensors designed for worst-case scenarios with excessive safety margins
  • Scheduled replacement: Fixed maintenance intervals based on statistical models rather than actual sensor condition
  • Multiple redundancy: High-trust applications deploy 2-3x sensors for the same function, including one-out-of-two (1oo2) sensor architectures[1]
  • No Fault Found (NFF): Research shows NFF events are "one of the major threats to overall reliability and customer satisfaction" in automotive[2]
  • Industry spends $50B annually on warranty claims[7]

Solution Overview

Core Value: Replace/augment static design-centric reliability with dynamic, intelligent monitoring that understands sensor physics, predicts failures, and optimizes performance in real-time.

  • Predictive Failure Detection: Identify degradation patterns before failure occurs
  • Real-Time Calibration: Maintain optimal sensor accuracy throughout lifecycle
  • Security Monitoring: Detect and mitigate spurious signals and cyber threats
  • Diagnostic Intelligence: Pinpoint root causes of sensor anomalies
  • Condition-Based Maintenance: Replace only when physics models indicate actual need

Technical Advantages

  • Physics-based models vs. simple statistical approaches
  • Real-time adaptation to changing conditions
  • Multi-sensor fusion for enhanced reliability
  • Edge computing for low-latency response
  • Quality optimization by identifying manufacturing defects early
  • Supply chain resilience through better failure prediction

Business Impact

  • Vehicle Operations: Reduction in unnecessary sensor replacements and diagnostic time
  • Enhanced Safety: Early fault detection and improved autonomy reliability
  • Manufacturing: Inventory reduction through demand-based procurement
  • Supply Chain: Streamlined logistics with predictive replacement scheduling
  • Revenue Streams: Software-as-a-Service and performance-based contracts
  • Platform Ecosystem: Connecting vehicles, suppliers, and service providers
