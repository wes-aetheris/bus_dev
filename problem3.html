The $10B Problem We're Solving

Silent sensor degradation, safety failures, and wasteful maintenance practices across critical industries

The Problem

Silent Sensor Degradation
Sensors fail without warning, providing incorrect data while appearing functional[1]
Schedule-Based Replacement
Functional components replaced on time/mileage schedules rather than actual condition[2][3]
Diagnostic Code Misinterpretation
Codes indicate sensor readings, not sensor failure, yet trigger unnecessary replacements[4][5]
Lack of Granular Monitoring
30-40% savings possible with condition-based maintenance[6][7]
Manufacturing
• Healthy sensors replaced during scheduled maintenance windows[2]
• Temperature and vibration sensors replaced on fixed schedules[8]
• 25% reduction in sensor maintenance costs with IoT monitoring[9]
Wind Energy
• Turbine sensors serviced every 2,500-5,000 hours regardless of sensor condition[10]
• Vibration and temperature sensors insufficient to detect early failures[11]
• $42K-48K annual sensor monitoring and replacement costs per MW[12]
Healthcare
• Medical device sensors replaced at fixed intervals regardless of performance[13]
• Pressure and flow sensors in pumps replaced despite remaining functional life[14]
• IoT sensor monitoring enables automated replacement when sensors near end of life[15]

Industry Impact

Defense Industry
• Military aircraft sensors: 40% of removals test "No Fault Found"[16]
• Avionics sensors experience up to 20% unnecessary replacement rates[17]
• Intermittent sensor faults cause $2B+ in annual NFF costs[18]
• Aging sensor connections create "soft faults" undetected by diagnostics[19]
Critical Automotive Sensors
• O2 Sensors: Replaced every 60K-100K miles[19]
• Crankshaft/Camshaft: Essential for operation, replaced preventively[20][21]
• Throttle Position: Critical for fuel control, replaced on codes[22]
• MAF Sensors: 98% failures in electronics, full units replaced[23]
Autonomous Vehicles
• Disengagements from improper sensor readings[24]
• Map and calibration data errors[25]
• 75% of "software" failures are sensor-related[26]
Drone Operations
• 100x higher sensor failure rate than manned aircraft[27]
• 50% of mishaps from sensor and component failures[27]
• 20% of sensor maintenance actions could be avoided[28]

Financial Impact

Direct Costs
• $200-500 per automotive sensor replacement[29]
• Defense sensor maintenance: $2B+ annual NFF costs[18]
• $42K-48K annual wind sensor costs per MW[12]
Potential Savings
• 30-40% savings with sensor condition-based maintenance[6][7]
• 25-50% reduction in sensor-related downtime possible[30]
• 25% reduction in sensor maintenance costs with IoT[9]
